    Rules

  • Blackjack is usually played with one or more standard decks of 52 cards.

  • The goal is to beat the dealer by having a hand value as close to 21 as possible without exceeding it.

  • Number cards (2â€“10) are worth their face value; face cards (Jack, Queen, King) are worth 10 points.

  • Aces can be worth either 1 or 11 points, whichever benefits the hand more.

  • Each player is dealt two cards face up, while the dealer receives one face up and one face down.

  • A "Blackjack" is when a player's initial hand is an Ace and a 10-point card, totaling exactly 21.

  • Players can choose to "Hit" to take another card or "Stand" to end their turn.

  • If a player's hand exceeds 21 after a hit, they "Bust" and lose the round.

  • Once all players have finished, the dealer reveals their hidden card and must hit until reaching at least 17.

  • If the dealer busts, all remaining players win.

  • If neither busts, the hand closest to 21 wins. A tie results in a "Push" where the bet is returned.
